Seen & Strong (SS)
A wellbeing encouragement app
Tagline:
You are seen. You are heard. You are stronger than this moment.
Mission:
To provide gentle, personalised encouragement to people struggling with anxiety, sadness, loneliness, or emotional pain, helping them rediscover their worth and take small steps toward healing.

1. Target Users
People who:
•	feel anxious
•	struggle with depression
•	feel rejected or unseen
•	are overwhelmed with life
•	need encouragement
•	want a safe emotional space
Age range: 16+

2. Core Idea of the App
A user opens the app and enters:
•	their name
•	how they feel
•	the problem they are facing
•	optional age range
The app then generates a personalised encouragement message that:
•	acknowledges their pain
•	reminds them of their worth
•	offers hope
•	gives one small step forward

3. App Structure
Screen 1 — Welcome Screen
Soft calming background.
Text:
Welcome to Seen & Strong
This is a safe place for encouragement and hope.
You are not invisible.
You are not alone.
Take a moment and share what you are going through.
Buttons:
Start

Screen 2 — User Input
Fields:
Name
Example: Michael
How do you feel today?
Dropdown:
•	Sad
•	Anxious
•	Angry
•	Overwhelmed
•	Lonely
•	Tired
•	Hopeless
•	Just need encouragement
What challenge are you facing today?
Text box:
Example:
I feel like I keep failing in life.
Optional: Age range
•	Under 18
•	18–25
•	26–40
•	41–60
•	60+
Button:
Encourage Me

4. Response Screen
Example response:
Michael, thank you for sharing this.
Feeling like you are failing can be very painful, especially when you try your best and things still seem difficult. But your worth is not measured only by your struggles or setbacks.
You are still growing, still learning, and still capable of creating a meaningful life. The fact that you are here shows that you have not given up. That is strength.
Remember this: a difficult chapter does not mean your story is over.
Today, take one small step — breathe slowly three times and remind yourself:
“I am still here. My story is still being written.”
Buttons:
•	Another Message
•	Small Step for Today

5. Small Step Feature
The app suggests simple actions:
Examples:
Take 3 slow breaths
Drink a glass of water
Step outside for fresh air
Write one sentence about your feelings
Text someone you trust
Stretch your body for 2 minutes
Listen to calming music
Purpose:
Encouragement + action.

6. Daily Strength Feature
Every day the user receives a word.
Example:
Today's Strength Word: Courage
Courage is not the absence of fear.
It is choosing to keep going even when your heart feels heavy.

7. Mood Tracker
User selects mood daily.
Mood scale:
😊 Happy
🙂 Okay
😐 Neutral
😔 Sad
😞 Struggling
Weekly summary:
You checked in 5 times this week.
That shows commitment to your wellbeing.

8. Encouragement Library
Users can explore categories:
•	Anxiety
•	Loneliness
•	Self-worth
•	Healing
•	Confidence
•	Strength
•	Starting again
Each contains short encouragement messages.

9. Voice Encouragement
User presses:
Read Message Aloud
A calm voice reads the encouragement.
Very powerful for users who feel emotionally overwhelmed.

10. Emergency Support System
If a user types something like:
"I want to die"
"I want to hurt myself"
The app responds with:
I'm really sorry that you are hurting this deeply.
You deserve immediate support from a real person who can help you through this moment.
Please contact a trusted support service now.
Options displayed:
•	Call NHS 111
•	Call Samaritans 116 123
•	Contact emergency services

11. Personal Affirmation Generator
User types their name.
Example:
Sarah's Affirmation
Sarah, you are not defined by your hardest days.
You are someone who is still standing, still learning, and still worthy of love.
